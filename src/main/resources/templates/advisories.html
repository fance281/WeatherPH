Travel Safety Advisories for the Philippines

Navigating the Philippines requires vigilance against its dynamic weather. From sudden tropical downpours to intense heat and typhoons, conditions can change rapidly. This guide provides essential safety tips for common hazards to help you plan safer journeys.

üåÄ Typhoon & Strong Winds

Typhoons (Bagyo) are the most significant weather threat. Understanding PAGASA's Public Storm Warning Signals (PSWS) is critical.

Signal Wind Speed Impact & Precaution
Signal #1 39-61 km/h Minimal threat. Be aware. Sea travel for small seacraft is risky.
Signal #2 62-88 km/h Moderate threat. Unsafe for small seacraft. Postpone non-essential travel.
Signal #3 89-117 km/h Serious threat. Widespread disruption likely. Avoid all travel.
Signal #4 118-184 km/h Severe threat. Heavy damage expected. Stay indoors in a safe location.
Signal #5 >185 km/h Extreme threat. Catastrophic damage. Evacuation may be necessary.
