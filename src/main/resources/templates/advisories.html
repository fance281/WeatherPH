Weather & Road Hazard Advisories

Travel in the Philippines means dealing with quickly-changing weather and unpredictable road conditions. Intense rain and typhoons can flood streets in minutes. Landslides, falling debris, or accidents can create new hazards during storms. Extreme heat may cause vehicle problems and impact health.

Current Safety Tips

Heavy Rain / Flooding

  • Check PAGASA and LGU alerts before travel.
  • Avoid flood-prone, low-lying roads and underpasses.
  • Do not attempt to cross flooded streets‚ÄîTurn Around, Don't Drown.
  • Bring emergency supplies in case of sudden heavy rain.

Strong Winds / Typhoon

  • Secure outdoor belongings that could be blown away.
  • Avoid travel during active typhoon/bagyo warnings.
  • Stay off coastal roads during storm surge advisories.
  • Keep tuned to PAGASA bulletins in your area.

Extreme Heat

  • Limit outdoor travel midday; hydrate frequently.
  • Check temperature/heat index before heading out.
  • Never leave pets, kids, or elders in vehicles‚Äîrisk of heatstroke.

Road Hazards & Travel Safety

  • Check for road closures, flood, landslide or accident advisories before departing.
  • Use alternate routes if hazards are reported.
  • Maintain safe speed and distance, especially in hazardous weather.
  • Report road problems/obstructions to authorities or LGU hotlines.
